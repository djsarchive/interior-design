Scandinavian Style

In a Scandinavian interior, you'll often see a neutral color palette, with white, gray, and beige being the most commonly used colors. This creates a clean and uncluttered look that allows the natural materials to take center stage. Wood is a key element of Scandinavian design, with light woods like birch, pine, and ash being used for furniture, flooring, and decorative accents.

Mid-Century Modern Style

Mid-century modern interior design is a style that emerged in the 1950s and 1960s, characterized by its clean lines, organic shapes, and minimal ornamentation. It features iconic pieces of furniture like the Eames lounge chair and the Saarinen tulip table, and often incorporates bright, bold colors and graphic patterns. Mid-century modern interiors have a timeless, classic feel that has endured over the years.

Bohemian Style

Bohemian interior design is a style that celebrates creativity, free-spiritedness, and individuality. It's all about mixing and matching different colors, patterns, and textures to create a unique and eclectic space that reflects your personality and style. This style draws inspiration from bohemian fashion, which is known for its unconventional and artistic approach.

Art Deco Style

Art deco interior design is a style that emerged in the 1920s and 1930s, characterized by its bold geometric shapes, metallic accents, and opulent materials like marble and velvet. Art deco interiors often feature bold colors like black, gold, and red, and incorporate luxurious details like mirrored surfaces, crystal chandeliers, and decorative molding. Art deco interiors have a glamorous, sophisticated feel that is both timeless and modern.
